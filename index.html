Biography

I am Yuxiang Zhang, currently an associate professor at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT). I serve as a member of the Antennas and Radio Frequency Committee and the Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface Technology Technical Committee of the China Institute of Communications, as well as the Youth Working Committee of the Beijing Institute of Communications. I received my B.Eng. degree from Dalian University of Technology in 2014 and my Ph.D. degree from BUPT in 2020. During my doctoral study, I was a jointly trained Ph.D. student at the University of Waterloo, Canada, from 2018 to 2019. From 2020 to 2023, I conducted postdoctoral research at BUPT.

My research interests include wireless channel modeling and prediction for mobile communications and digital twin. I have published more than 60 SCI/EI-indexed papers, including 1 ESI Highly Cited Paper and 3 Hot Papers, and have been granted more than 10 invention patents. I have served as Principal Investigator for projects including the Young Scientists Fund of the NSFC and the National Key R&D Program of China, and have also participated in other projects including the NSFC, the National Key R&D Program of China and National Science and Technology Major Projects. More than 30 technical proposals have been adopted by international standardization organizations such as 3GPP and ITU, leading to the development of one 3GPP international standard.

Research Interests

  2. AI Foundation Models, Intelligent Agents, and Embodied Intelligence for Channel and Network Digital Twins
