Biography

Yuxiang Zhang is an Associate Professor at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications. Current academic service includes membership in the Antennas and Radio Frequency Committee of the China Institute of Communications, the Intelligent Metasurface Technology Committee, and the Youth Working Committee of the Beijing Institute of Communications. Long-term research focuses on wireless channel modeling and prediction for mobile communications, as well as digital twin technologies. More than 60 SCI/EI-indexed papers have been published in related areas, including 1 ESI Highly Cited Paper and 3 Hot Papers, together with more than 10 granted invention patents. Recognition includes selection for the Beijing Association for Science and Technology Young Talent Support Program and the title of “6G Rising Star Young Scholar” at the Global 6G Conference.

Research leadership includes principal investigation of projects such as the Young Scientists Fund of the National Natural Science Foundation of China and subprojects under the National Key Research and Development Program. Participation has also covered more than 10 national-, provincial-, and ministerial-level research projects, including those supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the National Key Research and Development Program, and National Science and Technology Major Projects. More than 30 technical contributions have been adopted by international standardization organizations such as 3GPP and ITU, contributing to one 3GPP international standard.

Research achievements have been recognized by the Second Prize of the Science and Technology Progress Award of the China Institute of Communications in 2025 as first contributor. Additional distinctions as a major contributor include the First Prize of the Science and Technology Progress Award of the Chinese Institute of Electronics in 2025, the First Prize of the Technology Invention Award of the China Institute of Communications in 2021, the First Prize of the Electronic Information Teaching Achievement Competition of the Chinese Institute of Electronics in 2025, the Gold Medal at the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva, and the IEEE ICCC 2025 Best Demonstration Award.

Research Interests

  1. Wireless channel measurement and modeling theory for complex space–air–ground–sea integrated scenarios toward sixth-generation mobile communications
  2. AI foundation models, intelligent agents, and embodied intelligence for wireless channel and network digital twins
  3. Wireless channel emulation and testing methodologies for future mobile communication systems and space–air–ground–sea integrated communication systems
