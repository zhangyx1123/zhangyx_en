Biography

Yuxiang Zhang is an Associate Professor at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications. He serves as a committee member of the Antennas and Radio Frequency Committee of the China Institute of Communications, the Intelligent Metasurface Technology Committee, and the Youth Working Committee of the Beijing Institute of Communications. His research has long focused on wireless channel modeling, prediction, and digital-twin-enabled technologies for mobile communications. More than 60 SCI/EI-indexed papers have been published in related areas, including 1 ESI Highly Cited Paper and 3 Hot Papers. He holds more than 10 authorized invention patents, and has been selected for the Beijing Association for Science and Technology Young Talent Support Program and honored as a “6G Rising Star Young Scholar” at the Global 6G Conference.

He has served as Principal Investigator for projects including the Young Scientists Fund of the National Natural Science Foundation of China and sub-projects of the National Key Research and Development Program, while also participating in more than 10 national and ministerial/provincial-level projects, such as programs supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the National Key Research and Development Program, and national major science and technology initiatives. More than 30 technical contributions have been adopted by international standardization bodies including 3GPP and ITU, resulting in 1 3GPP international standard.

As first contributor, he received the Second Prize of the 2025 Science and Technology Progress Award from the China Institute of Communications. As a major contributor, he also received the First Prize of the 2025 Science and Technology Progress Award from the Chinese Institute of Electronics, the First Prize of the 2021 Technology Invention Award from the China Institute of Communications, the First Prize of the 2025 Electronic Information Teaching Achievement Competition of the Chinese Institute of Electronics, the Gold Medal at the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva, and the IEEE ICCC 2025 Best Demonstration Award.

Research Interests

  1. Wireless channel measurement and modeling theory for complex space-air-ground-sea scenarios toward sixth-generation mobile communications
  2. AI foundation models, intelligent agents, and embodied intelligence for wireless channel and network digital twins
  3. Wireless channel emulation and testing methodologies for future mobile communication systems and space-air-ground-sea integrated communication systems
